John Ambrose II is an American football quarterback for the Baltimore Ravens of the National Football League. He was drafted with the last pick of the 2024 NFL draft and has played for the Ravens for his entire career. Ambrose II is the son of former Bills quarterback, and current Cleveland Browns Offensive Coordinator, John Ambrose. Ambrose II attended Rosewood State University. Ambrose won two Super Bowl MVP awards, 3 pro bowl nods, 2 First-Team All-Pro selections, 3 NFL MVP awards, 2 OPOY awards, and is currently ranked as the fourth greatest quarterback of all time. Ambrose was the 2024 Mr. Irrelevant, which is a joke award given to the last overall pick of each NFL draft.

1 Early Life[ | ]

Ambrose was born to John Ambrose and Amanda Jeong on November 11th 2003.[1] He grew up in the affluent Northwest neighborhood in Washington D.C. Ambrose attended Sidwell Friends, a private Quaker school from kindergarten to high school. Ambrose grew up as a life-long Ravens fan, and dreamed of playing for his favorite team. John is the oldest of three siblings.

2 High School Career[ | ]

Ambrose made the varsity football team in his sophomore season, in which he was a backup for the majority of the season. He did play in 4 games, where he passed for 678 yards, and had 6 touchdowns to 2 interceptions. In his junior season, Ambrose was out with an ACL tear for the entire season. He would enter physical therapy in the summer offseason.

Ambrose came back for his senior season as the starting quarterback, and led the Sidwell Friends Foxes to a Mid-Atlantic Athletic Conference title. In the championship game, Ambrose threw for 300 yards, and had 100 yards rushing, as well as 3 passing TDs and 2 rushing TDs. Overall, Ambrose threw for 4,456 yards, 28 TDs, and 4 interceptions, and was named player of the year. Ambrose also competed in track and field, where he won 2 consecutive state championships, and was named the Montgomery County Athlete of the Year, and the Maryland Athlete of the Year.

2.1 Recruiting[ | ]

Ambrose was the highest ranked Quarterback prospect for the District of Columbia. Overall he was fairly highly ranked, being listed as the 17th best quarterback in the country. Scouts noted his incredible athletic abilities as a runner, but had questions regarding his ability to read the defense, and throw an accurate football. Other scouts and analysts believed that Ambrose should switch positions to wide receiver or running back.

By January of 2022, Ambrose received offers from Maryland, Virginia Tech, Virginia, Towson, UCLA, and Kansas, however Kansas Towson, and Virginia were immediately ruled out as those programs would've required Ambrose to switch positions to wide receiver or running back. In Feburary, Ambrose narrowed the list to conference rivals Maryland, and Rosewood State. Ambrose ultimately chose Rosewood State over Maryland thanks to a promise from the program's representatives that he'd be guaranteed playing time as a freshman.

3 College Career[ | ]

Ambrose majored in Psychology, however he did not complete his bachelor's degree due to his decision to opt out of his senior season and enter the 2024 NFL draft.

3.1 Freshman Season[ | ]

In his freshman season, Ambrose took the Rosas from a 5-7 team to a 8-4 team, narrowly losing the ACC Capital Division Championship to Maryland 10-13. He threw for 3,669 yards, had 21 touchdowns and 11 interceptions. Ambrose also came close to beating the Rosewood State record for rushing yards in a single game by a quarterback. A record that was set by Rosewood State Hall of Famer Scott Odom, when in week 4 of the 2005 season, where Odom rushed for over 300 yards. Ambrose was 10 yards short tying the record with 290 yards in week 15 against #3 UCLA.

3.2 Sophomore Season[ | ]

Ambrose's sophomore season saw him continuing to progress as a passer. He rushed for 1,050 yards, and threw for 5,111 yards, setting the record for the most rushing and passing yards by a quarterback. Ambrose led the Rosas to an 11-1 record, and to the National Championship; where the Rosas would dominate the Louisville Cardinals 56-3. Ambrose was named the 2026 Rose Bowl MVP, and also won the Heisman Trophy. He would also earn the team MVP award and lead the ACC in rushing and passing yards.

3.3 Junior Season[ | ]

In his final season at Rosewood State, Ambrose threw for over 4,500 yards and 28 touchdowns to only 7 interceptions. He had an additionally had another thousand yard rushing season, making it three consecutive 1,000+ yard rushing seasons. In the Division Championship, Ambrose threw 2 interceptions, the second of which cost the Rosas a trip to the ACC Championship game. This marked the first year that the Rosas failed to make the ACC Championship since 2020.

In the offseason, Ambrose announced that he'd be entering the 2027 NFL Draft.

5.2 Scouting[ | ]

Ambrose was considered the best quarterback prospect out of the 2027 draft class. Though some remained skeptical of Ambrose's ability to read a defense and pass the ball accurately down the field, especially on deep throws under pressure, Overall, it was believed that Ambrose would be taken somewhere in the middle of the first round of the draft. This was further supported by numerous anonymous reports from multiple organizations' scouts stating that interviews were quite positive. In press interviews, Ambrose stressed that he did not care where he was selected to, or when, and that he'd be happy to be given an opportunity to prove himself in the pros.

Ambrose would score above average on the Wonderlic, and clocked in at an astounding 4.33s 40-yard dash time. This would boost his stock before the draft began, and many in the public and in the media believed that Ambrose, while being a raw talent had the potential of going first overall.

5.3 Drafting[ | ]

To the surprise of many, Ambrose was passed on by the Los Angeles Rams, who initially expressed interest in Ambrose. Other teams who expressed interest but passed on Ambrose included: Falcons, Jets, and Raiders. Ambrose was ultimately taken with the last pick in the 2027 NFL draft to the Baltimore Ravens. This was a controversial pick on the part of the Ravens as they already had Lamar Jackson, who was similar in skillset, and ability, and who had already taken the Ravens to six Super Bowl Championships since 2020. When asked about this pick, Jackson stated his intentions to remain as the starting quarterback, however he also acknowledged the opportunity to teach a rookie, and prepare him for the future.

The reaction to this choice was mixed, especially amongst Ravens fans. Some believed that picking up another QB with a similar skillset was a good idea, whilst also remarking that Ambrose had the potential to be another generational talent just like Lamar Jackson. Other believed that this was a waste of a pick, that could've been used to bolster core of aging defensive backs and that selecting a quarterback, even with the final pick was disrespectful to Jackson and his legacy in Baltimore.

6 Professional Career[ | ]

As of the 2033 season, Ambrose has spent his entire career with the Baltimore Ravens.

6.1 2024 Season[ | ]

Ambrose sat out for most of his rookie season behind Lamar Jackson. Ambrose played in a handful of games, and started in the final game of the season. He ended up completing all of 11 of his passes in the game he started. He threw for only 93 yards and had a passer rating of 101.9. On the ground, Ambrose did not impress, having just a single carry for 10 yards.

6.2 2025 Season[ | ]

In his sophomore season, Ambrose started in half of the games due to Lamar Jackson missing much of the season due to injury.[2] Ambrose's first start of the season came against the Patriots in week 11, which was right after the Ravens' bye week. Ambrose threw for 188 yards, and had 45 yards rushing, completing 21 of his 39 (54%) of his passes. He had 3 touchdowns and no interception in the game for a passer rating of 92.7 . In the week 14, Ambrose completed 45-59 (76%) passes, and threw for over 281 yards, and had 5 touchdowns and 1 interception, which was good for a passer rating of 106.7 .

In total, Ambrose threw for over a thousand yards, and had 17 touchdowns to 5 interceptions, completed 66% of his passes and had a quarterback rating of 113.8. He would also lead the Ravens to 4 straight wins in the final stretch of the season, securing another playoff berth for the Ravens.[2]

In his first start in the playoffs, the Ravens faced the New York Jets. Ambrose passed for only 108 yards, and had 2 interceptions and only a single touchdown. However, Ambrose did show flashes of the rushing ability he displayed as a quarterback at Rosewood State. rushing for over 202 yards, and had 3 rushing touchdowns. In the third quarter, in particular, Ambrose scored the game tying touchdown after being down 14-21, following the a drive in which the Jets failed a 4th and 2 conversion at their 24 yard line. In the fourth quarter, Ambrose would revert back to struggling with 2 completions on 10 attempts, and an interception that tied the game back at 21 all. In the Divisional Round at the Raiders, Ambrose would run primarily RPOs or handoff the ball to avoid turning over the ball. He did complete 8 of 12 passes for 98 yards, and had a 28 yard touchdown pass to Marqusie Brown to end a game that the Ravens were already dominating in, with the final score being Ravens 38, Raiders 17. The AFC Championship game against the Bengals proved to be one of the worst performances passing for Ambrose in his career. He completed just 16 of 38 passes, threw for 117 yards, and had 2 touchdowns and 4 interceptions. The game ended with the Bengals making a 16 yard chipshot field goal following a fumble by HB Nate Wroten.[2]

6.3 2026 Season[ | ]

Going into his third season in the NFL, the media was divided on Ambrose. Some analysts argued that saw his potential, and argued that he'd improve over time, while others thought that Ambrose was already a bust citing his poor passing performance and lack of production on the ground. Statistically, Ambrose failed to impress, passing for only 1,200 yards, and had 17 touchdowns, though he did have the fewest interceptions with only a single interception. Ambrose sustained an PCL tear in week 10 of the seaon, and was out for the remainder of the season, being put on injured reserve.[3]

6.4 2027 Season[ | ]

In 2027, Ambrose returned to start, however he still split playing time with veteran QB Lamar Jackson.[3] He did not play for most of the season. He made his debut in week 14, when Lamar Jackson went down with a shoulder injury in week 14 of the regular season at home against the Cincinnati Bengals. Ambrose threw for 101 yards, 1 touchdown in the second half of the game. He was named the starting quarterback after Jackson was officially ruled out for the rest of the 2027 season.[3]

6.4.1 Regular Season[ | ]

In week 15 Ambrose led the Ravens to a 23-17 win against the Los Angeles Rams, throwing for 211 yards, and throwing 3 touchdowns and no picks.[3] Ambrose completed 76% of his passes, and was named AFC Offensive Player of the Week for his efforts.[3] The following week against the New England Patriots, Ambrose would bring the Ravens back from a 17-31 deficit in the second half, throwing for 301 yards, 3 touchdowns, as well as having 107 rushing yards and a rushing touchdown. In the final week of the regular season, Ambrose threw for 4 touchdowns, had a 61% completion percentage, and rushed for 188 yards, but the Ravens lost 25-24 in OT against the Cleveland Browns.[3]

6.4.2 Postseason[ | ]

The Ravens faced their division rivals, the Cincainnti Bengals in Baltimore. Ambrose thew for 256 yards, had 85 rushing yards, 1 rushing touchdown and 2 passing touchdowns. Leading the Ravens to a 15-point win over the Bengals, with a score of 36-21. However, due to injuries on the offensive line, Ambrose was sacked 4 times, and fumbled the ball once at the Bengals 38 yard line.[4]

In the AFC Championship game, the Ravens narrowly beat the Broncos 17-10. The Ravens led the Broncos 10-7 by halftime, and in the third quarter both teams settled into the trenches and a stalemate ensued. Both teams had multiple 3 and outs throughout the third and fourth quarter, however the Broncos would go on to score a 51-yard game-tieing field goal with 6:45 left.[4] John Ambrose would lead the offense down to the 14 yard line on their next possession; however Ravens HB Nate Wroten fumbled the ball and poession of the ball was given back to the Broncos. With 4:20 seconds left, the Ravens defense would get the ball again thanks to multiple holding calls on the Broncos offensive line, as well as a rare false start call on the offensive players except for the center. With 3:57 left on the clock, Baltimore would drive down the field with a 52 yard rush by Ambrose and the Ravens would score with 40 seconds left, sealing a trip to the Super Bowl.[4]

6.4.3 Super Bowl LXII[ | ]

In a shocking turn of events, Ambrose led the 15-1 Baltimore Ravens to 2 wins and 1 loss in the final 3 weeks of the season, and two playoff wins to land the Ravens in the Super Bowl.[4] In Super Bowl LXIII, Ambrose threw for 218 yards, and had 2 touchdowns to 1 interception, as well as 216 rushing yards and 3 rushing touchdowns. Ambrose was sacked 3 times by the Panthers defense.[4] Once again, the Ravens were tied with their opponent going into the fourth quarter, which saw neither team able to finish the game with a touchdown or with a field goal.[4] Ambrose threw the a 75 yard pass to Marquise Brown winning his first Super Bowl in the final two minutes of the game, sealing a 55-21 win, and making history as the first rookie Quarterback to win a Super Bowl. Ambrose was also named the Super Bowl MVP.[4]

6.5 2028 Season[ | ]

Ambrose was named the starter by coach Amallah in the 3rd week of the preseason, once the official ruling was made that Lamar Jackson would be out for the remainder of the season in order to receive surgery for his hip.[5] The 2028 season was Ambrose's first season as the full time starting quarterback.[5] Ambrose passed for over 4,500 yards and had 2,154 rushing yards. Week 3 was a rematch of the 2027 Divisional Round against the Cincinnati Bengals.[5] Ambrose threw for 98 yards, and had one touchdown to three interceptions, and rushed for only 54 yards on the way to a crushing 14-44 loss.[5] The next week saw Ambrose bounce back with a solid 255 passing yards, 3 touchdowns, 1 rushing touchdown and even 1 receiving touchdown against the Cleveland Browns.[5] In which the Ravens would win 56-24.[5]

The Ravens would fall to 6-10 on the season, and placed 3rd in the AFC North.[5] This was the first time that the Ravens had missed the postseason since the acquisition of the team by Hyojin Choi in 2020. Ambrose has another impressive season with 44 touchdowns to only 8 interceptions and was unanimously named MVP, which was only the third time that a player had been unanimously chosen for the honor. Ambrose also made his first ever Pro Bowl, and was selected to be a First Team All-Pro.[5]

6.6 2029 Season[ | ]

The 2029 season saw Ambrose once again splitting starts with veteran QB Lamar Jackson.[6] Despite, being listed as the backup QB, Ambrose played in 10 of the 16 games in the season. His first game of the season was in week 6, at home vs the Miami Dolphins. The Ravens fell behind 7-3 in the third quarter and starter Lamar Jackson threw 3 interceptions, 1 of which was a pick six.[6] Ambrose primary served as a pocket quarterback for the majority of the season in the hopes that he'd further progress as a passing quarterback.[6] Ambrose threw for 116 yards, had 1 touchdown, and completed 55% of his passes. The 2029 season was the first season Ambrose posted perfect passer rating of 158.3.

In the fourth quarter, the Ravens re-gained possession with 11 minutes left in the game.[6] Ambrose took the Ravens to the 16 yard line with 8 minutes left; however two consecutive incompeltions, and a rush by Youssef Amallah XIII for no gain caused the Ravens to settle for a field goal. The Dolphins would successfully go for two, and the score was 16-3.[6] With 5 minutes left, the Ravens were behind 16-6. Ambrose was put back into the game, primarily serving as an option QB. Ambrose had a 41 yard rush putting the Ravens into the red zone with 3:30 left. Coach Amallah would opt for a no-huddle offense, and the Ravens running backs would go down the field and score on a 5 play drive, with the final play of the drive being a 5 yard FB dive by Tavon Wiley.[6] The Ravens would elect to attempt an onside kick and was able to recover the ball right before the 2 minute warning, and having narrowed the gap to 10-13.[6]

6.7 2030 Season[ | ]

Ambrose did not play much of the 2030 season as well. He was set as the backup for Lamar Jackson for the majority of the season. He passed for only 430 yards, and had 4 touchdowns with no interceptions, which was good for a quarterback passer rating of 135.8, which was a bit of a step down from his perfect passer rating last season. Ambrose also rushed for only 36 yards and had four rushing touchdowns. Ambrose did start in the last game of the season, where he threw for 351 yards, 2 touchdowns and 1 interception. He would be placed back on the bench for the remainder of the season as a result of Lamar Jackson once again returning to start and play in the playoffs.

6.7.1 Contract Extension[ | ]

By the end of the 2030 season, Ambrose's contract was up. Negotiations began in the offfseason in 2029, however no concrete deal was made. During the 2030 season, the expectation was that Ambrae would sign a large extension due to him taking the Ravens to the Super Bowl for the second time.

Ambrose would go on to sign an 5 year, $189 million dollar contract with the Ravens in the offseason.[citation needed]

6.8 2031 Season[ | ]

2031 proved to be a bounceback season for Ambrose. He threw for over 4,000 yards, had 54 touchdowns to 20 interceptions, and completed over 70% of his passes. He was once again named to the All-Pro team, and was named to the Pro Bowl for the second time in his career. Ambrose was also given the Quarterback of the Year award.

The worst performance of the season came from week 14, at home against the Bengals. Ambrose threw only a single touchdown in the game in the first quarter. The only other points in the game came from a Younghoe Koo field goal in the 3rd quarter. The game ended in overtime with a tie, which was the first, and only tie in his career. In the Divisional Round, Ambrose would post historic numbers for rushing by a quarterback. He had over 256 yards rushing, and 5 rushing touchdowns, in a game the Ravens would go on to win dominantly over the Bengals 54-27. Ambrose continued this success in the AFC Championship game where he posted 291 yards passing, 204 yards rushing, completed 31-37 passes and had 3 passing and 1 rushing touchdown.

Ambrose's solid performance passing took the Ravens to the their 11th Super Bowl,, where he would pass for 194 yards, 2 touchdowns, 1 rushing touchdown, and completed 26-33 of his passes for a passer rating of 111.4 , and was Super Bowl MVP for the first time in his career.

6.9 2032 Season[ | ]

Following his dominate season in 2031, Ambrose showed further improvement and in training camp. He had taken the Ravens to the playoffs in all but one season he started, and was quickly becoming the best quarterback in the league. As a result, coach Amallah and Hyojin Choi made the controversial decision to trade longtime starting QB Lamar Jackson to the Washington Senators in exchange for a 2 first round picks, 2 second round picks, 1 future first round pick, and a conditional 3rd round pick. This decision would initially sparked outrage amongst Ravens fans, as Lamar Jackson had been and a longtime favorite in Baltimore.

Ambrose proved to the fans why he should start when in second game of the preseason, the Ravens were down by 17 against the Chargers with 10 minutes left in the game. Ambrose's first drive in the second half was a one play drive, where the threw 66 yards to Jerry Rice for a touchdown. On the next drive, the Ravens got the ball on the 27 yard line, and drove down the field with 5 minutes left in the game, and went on to score on a 13 yard run into the end zone off a run-pass option (RPO). The Chargers would score on their next drive, to bring the deficit back up to 10. The Ravens would get to midfield, before stalling out and failing to make a 1st down. However, the coach Amallah would opt to go for it with 4th down and 13 yards to go. The Ravens would run another RPO and Ambrose would throw a completed pass to Gio Pearson at the Chargers' 40 yard line. Then, the Ravens would go no-huddle and finished the drive scoring with 2 minutes to go, and with the deficit back down to a field goal. Baltimore choose to do an onside kick, which they recovered. Ambrose completed 10 of his 18 passes, and rushed for 11 yards, taking the Ravens into the red zone with a minute left. On the final play of the game, Ambrose handed the ball to Youssef Amallah who got the touchdown sealing the win for the Ravens.

6.10 2033 Season[ | ]

2033 was another solid season for Ambrose; he would throw for over 4,000 yards and led the league in quarterback rushing yards by a quarterback with 1,287 yards. He threw 53 touchdowns to only 8 interceptions, completed 70% of his passes and once again had a passer rating of over 100. His incredible regular season performance was contrasted by his somewhat lacking postseason performance, where he posted only 151 yards, 4 touchdowns and 2 interceptions. Ambrose completed only 55% of his passes in the postseason, and only 41% in the AFC Championship game against Cincinnati.

6.11 2034 Season[ | ]

The 2034 season is Ambrose's 11th in the NFL and with the Baltimore Ravens. In the first week, the took the Ravens to a 63-19 win over the New Orleans Saints. Ambrose threw for 279, completed 17 passes on 24 attempts, and ahd 4 touchdowns and no interceptions. He also rushed for 252 yards on 20 carries, with an average of 12.4 yards per carry. He had 4 rushing touchdowns and 1 fumble. Ambrose was named AFC Offensive Player of the Week for the 29th time in his career, and continuing a streak of being named AFC Offensive Player of the Week for 8 consecutive weeks. Ambrose would follow that up with a win over the Cleveland Browns. Ambrose threw for 324 yards, 3 passing TDs, completed 16-25, and had 243 rushing yards and a touchdown. For his efforts, Ambrose was once again named AFC Offensive Player of the Week.

11 Political Beliefs[ | ]

11.1 Black Lives Matter Movement[ | ]

Ambrose has voiced his support for the Black Lives Matter movement, and for kneeling during the National Anthem to protest police brutality. He also been seen multiple times wearing masks with the phrase: "I can't breathe," in reference to the killings of Eric Garner, Elijah McClain, and George Floyd. In addition, he has donated $556,000 to the Black Lives Matter organization, and to various other organizations trying to redistribute police funds to mental health resources and other community works projects.

12 Philanthropy[ | ]

Ambrose has worked with his father John Ambrose's charity the Ambrose Foundation, which provides scholarships to at-risk, and low-income students in the Washington D.C. Metropolitan Area (DC Metro).

13 Personal Life[ | ]

Ambrose grew up Christian, and is a practicing Christian; however he has stated that he does not affiliate with any particular denomination. Ambrose married his longtime girlfriend, Kyra on May 16th, 2030, On November 22nd, 2031, the two had their first child named Aaliyah, and are expecting their second child in September of 2034

Ambrose is a member, and partial owner of the Union City Lawn Tennis, Golf, and Racquet Club. He was the first person of color to ever have partial ownership of the club. John is the older brother of Dante Ambrose, who is a rapper, songwriter, and activist; as well as the older brother of Ashlee, who is currently serving as a member of the House of Delegates for Maryland's 19th House of Delegates district, and his youngest sibling is brother, Cam, who is also an NFL quarterback.
