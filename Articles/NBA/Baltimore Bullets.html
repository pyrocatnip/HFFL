The Baltimore Bullets are a professional American basketball team based in Baltimore, Maryland. The Bulllets compete in the American Basketball Association (ABA) as a member of the Eastern Conference Atlantic Division. The team currently plays in M&T Bank Arena, located in Baltimore's inner harbor.

The franchise was founded in 2020, and is named after the Baltimore Bullets, who played in the American Basketball Association from (ABA) from 1963 until 1973.

Franchise History

2021-22: Inaugural Season

The Baltimore Bullets were announced as the first expansion franchise in the ABA's new ABA 2.0 project. The ABA chose Baltimore due to the rabbid faABAse, and the history of sports in the city. The Bullets joined the Eastern Conference Atlantic Division, along with their new rivals in the Washington Wizards. In their first season, the Bullets assembled a decent squad surrounded by the undrafted free agent Hyojin Choi from South Korea. The Bullets would suprise the entire league in a strike shortened season, going 15-2 and winning the Eastern Conference. Choi ended the season with 19.6 points per game, 13.8 rebounds, 7.8 assists, and made 98% of his field goals, and was named to the first team All-ABA and the All-Star Roster. The Bullets would end the season losing to the eventual champion Indiana Pacers in the conference semifinals.

2021-22: First ABA Title

Main article: 2021-22 Baltimore Bullets Season

In their second season, the Bullets would sign undrafted free agents Jarius Lyles and Scott Odom from the University of Maryland Baltimore County. Alongside Choi, the Bullets would start the season tearing up the league. Early on the in season, the Bullets would also make the decision to part ways with veteran star LeBron James, who was traded to the Bullets' rival, the Washington Wizards. The Bullets would also sign Austin Versace from free agency, creating a young core that would ensure the longevity of the Bullets winning culture. The Bullets would also win their first ever ABA Championship, defeating the Hawaii Honu, 181-178.
