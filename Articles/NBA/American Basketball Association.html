The American Baskeball Association (ABA) is a professional basketball league in North America. The league is composed of 36 teams (34 in the United States and 2 in Canada) and is one of the major professional sports leagues in the United States and Canada. It is the premier men's basketball league in the world. In 2020, the ABA merged with the ABA, and all existing ABA franchises were moved to the ABA. During this merger the vast majority of the ABA's old franchises were re-located and re-branded, except for the Washington Wizards, and the Sacramento Kings (Now the Long Beach Kings).

The Baltimore Bullets are the defending league champions, as they defeated the San Francisco Royals 181-171 in the ABA Finals.

Structure

Ownership of all ABA franchises is public. This means that fans and players are able to purchase small shares in a franchise. Players are given a 5% share in a franchise, while fans are given a 0.1% share.

The league is operated by a Commissioner who oversees all administrative responsibilities for the league. The current league Commissioner is Theresa Goodwin. The League's Commissioner serves for a 5 year term, with no maximum number of terms set.

Regular Season

Following the summer break, teams begin tranning camps in late September. This period allows for coaching staffs to evalute their players, discover team strengths and weaknesses, prepare for the upcoming season, and to reduce the roster down to the mandatory 15 maximum players on the starting roster. This period also allows teams to send players with less than two years of experience to the G-League. After training camps end the Summer League begins, which is a series of exhibition games.

During the regular season, each team plays a total of 82 games, 41 at home and 41 away. Every team will face the other teams in their division four teams a season (16 games). Each team will also play six games against teams from the two other division in the conference (24 games), and the remaining four teams three times (12 games). Finally, each team plays all teams in the other conference twice (30 games). This structure is assymetrical which allows for variability in the strength of schedule, however this occurs at a lesser degree than that of the NFL or MLB. Over five seasons, each team will have played 80 games in division, (20 per opponent, 10 home, 10 away), 180 games against the result of their conference (18 total, 9 home, 9 on the road), and 150 games against their other conference (10 games against each team, 5 home, 5 away).

The ABA is the only one of the major American sports leagues to play games on Christmas Day. Games played on this day are usually the highest-rated games in a given season.

Typically in the middle of April, the regular season ends. During this time voting begins for individual awards, as well as league-wide honors. The Sixth-Man of the Year award is given to the best player off the bench, and has had more games coming off the bench than actual starts. The Rookie of the Year Award is given to the most outstanding first-year player in the league. The Most Improved Player Awards is given to the player who is believed to have had the most improvement from the previous season. The Defensive Player of the Year Awards is given to the player deemed the most valuable defender in the league. The Coach of the Year Award is given to the coach who has had the most positiveimpact in the league that season.The Most Valuable Player Award is given to the player deemed the most valuable for (his team) that season. Additionally, Sporting News awards an unofficial (but widely recognized) Executive of the Year Award to the general manager who is adjudged to have performed the best job for the benefit of his franchise.

The postseason teams are the All-ABA Team, the All-Defensive Team, and the All-Rookie Team; each consists of five players. There are three All-ABA teams, consisting of the top players at each position, with first-team status being the most desirable. There are two All-Defensive teams, consisting of the top defenders at each position. There are also two All-Rookie teams, consisting of the top first-year players regardless of position.

Playoffs

Main articles: ABA Championship Series, ABA National Championship

The ABA Championship Series begins in April after the conclusion of the regular season with the top eight teams in each conference, regardless of divisional alignment, competing for the league's championship title, the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy. Seeds are awarded in strict order of regular season record (with a tiebreaker system used as needed). The competition was known as the ABA Playoffs during the 2021-22 ABA season

Having a higher seed offers several advantages. Since the first seed begins the playoffs playing against the eighth seed, the second seed plays the seventh seed, the third seed plays the sixth seed, and the fourth seed plays the fifth seed, having a higher seed means a team faces a weaker team in the first round. The team in each series with the better record has home-court advantage, including the First Round. Before the league changed its playoff determination format for the 2006–07 season, this meant that, for example, if the team that received the sixth seed had a better record than the team with the third seed (by virtue of a divisional championship), the sixth seed would have home-court advantage, even though the other team had a higher seed. Therefore, the team with the best regular season record in the league is guaranteed home-court advantage in every series it plays. For example, in 2006, the Denver Nuggets won 44 games and captured the Northwest Division and the third seed. Their opponent was the sixth-seeded Los Angeles Clippers, who won 47 games and finished second in the Pacific Division. Although Denver won its much weaker division, the Clippers had a home-court advantage and won the series in 5.

The playoffs follow a tournament format. Each team plays an opponent in a best-of-seven series, with the first team to win four games advancing into the next round, while the other team is eliminated from the playoffs. In the next round, the successful team plays against another advancing team of the same conference. All but one team in each conference are eliminated from the playoffs. Since the ABA does not re-seed teams, the playoff bracket in each conference uses a traditional design, with the winner of the series matching the first- and eighth-seeded teams playing the winner of the series matching the fourth- and fifth-seeded teams, and the winner of the series matching the second- and seventh-seeded teams playing the winner of the series matching the third- and sixth-seeded teams. In every round, the best-of-7 series follows a 2–2–1–1–1 home-court pattern, meaning that one team will have home court in games 1, 2, 5, and 7, while the other plays at home in games 3, 4, and 6. From 1985 to 2013, the ABA Finals followed a 2–3–2 pattern, meaning that one team had home court in games 1, 2, 6, and 7, while the other played at home in games 3, 4, and 5.[99]

The final playoff round, a best-of-seven series between the victors of both conferences, is known as the ABA Finals and is held annually in June. The winner of the ABA National Championship receives the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy. Each player and major contributor—including coaches and the general manager—on the winning team receive a championship ring. In addition, the league awards the Bill Russell ABA Finals Most Valuable Player Award to the best performing player of the series.

The league began using its current format in 2020, where the top teams are seeded based on conference standings.
