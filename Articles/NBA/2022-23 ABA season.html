Up to date as of 2022-04-21

The 2022-23 ABA season will be the 2nd season of the American Basketball Association (ABA). The Baltimore Bullets are the defending ABA Champions. The regular season will begin in October, and will end in April of 2023. The NBA All-Star game will be played at United Healthcare Arena, Los Angeles, California. A play-in tournament for teams ranked 7th through 10th in each conference will be held in May of 2023. The ABA Playoffs will be held after that from late may into June of 2023.

The 2022-23 season also saw the creation of 5 additional new franchises entering their first season of play in the ABA. Those teams are the Detroit Wings, Louisville Knights, Memphis Skyhawks, Nashville Stars, Orlando Liberty, and the St. Louis Sound. The Louisville Knights joined the Eastern Conference Central Division. The Nashville Stars and the Orlando Liberty joined the Eastern Conference Southeast Division. The Memphis Skyhawks and the St. Louis Sound joined the Western Conference Southwest Division. An expansion draft was held for the newly founded teams on September 29th, 2022.

Rules Changes

  • Added expansion teams
  • Players are eligibile for the ABA draft once they are at the age of 18
  • The draft lottery was removed, making the draft order based purely on standings
  • Eliminated the shot clock
  • Back court violations will no longer be called
