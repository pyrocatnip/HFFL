List of Union City Red Devils head coaches

The Union City Red Devils have had four head coaches in franchise history. The Red Devils was a founding member of the United States Football League, they joined the Eastern Conference in the 2020 season. The Red Devils would then join the newly created Capital Division of the Eastern Conference in 2029.

Rick Hamilton was the first head coach in franchise history, leading the Red Devils from 2020 until 2028.

Head Coaches

Rick Hamilton (2020-2028)

Main: Rick Hamilton

Rick Hamilton served as the first head coach for the Red Devils. Hamilton had previously served as the Offensive Coordinator for the Virginia Tech Hokies since 2017. He held the position from the 2020 season until he was fired following an 0-10 start in the 2028 season. At the time of his firing, he had a record of 48-91-1, and Hamilton only took the team to a single Conference Championship prior to the expansion of the league into a 24 teams.

The 2020 season was the first season Hamilton served as the head coach of the Red Devils. He led the team to a disappointing 4-12 record, finishing 3rd in the Eastern Conference, and failing to make the playoffs. The next season Hamilton would improve by two games to a 6-10 record, once again placing 3rd in the Eastern Conference. In 2022, the team would again improve to an 8-8 record, still failing to make the playoffs. The 2023 season would prove to be a low point for the early history of the Red Devils. The team would go 3-13, and would place last in the Eastern Conference. Following this record, the Red Devils would earn the first overall draft pick. The next season was the high point during the Hamilton era. The team would go 10-6 and finished first in the Eastern Conference for the first time. This was the first time in franchise history that team would make it to the playoffs. In their first ever playoff game; which was also the National Championship, the Red Devils lost in 37-31 with a 99-yard punt return on the first play of overtime to the Miami Sun.

Following their heartbreaking loss in their first ever playoff apperance, the Red Devils would fail to make the playoffs during the remainder of the Hamilton era from 2025 until his firing in 2028. The team would go 7-9 in the 2025 season, and then collapsed, going 4-12 and then 5-11 in Hamilton's final two full seasons as head coach. Going into the 2028 season pressure began to mount and rumors of a possible firing if the team failed to perform emerged to the media during the offseason. The rumors would come true as Hamilton would be fired following an 0-10 start to the 2028 season, with Offensive Coordinator Allen Sexton promoted to interim head coach.

Hamilton is currently serving as the head coach for the Alabama Crimson Tide.

Allen Sexton (Interim) (2028-2030)

Allen Sexton served as the interim head coach during the process of finding a more permanent head coach. Sexton has previously been serving as the Offensive Coordinator for the Red Devils since the inaugural season in 2020. Sexton would lead the Red Devils to a 14-24 record, and failing to make the postseason during his entire two and half season tenure at head coach. Sexton would resign following the announcement of the hiring of Union City legend, Scott Odom as the next head coach for the Red Devils.

Sexton would then serve as the Offensive Coordinator of the Tampa Bay Vipers of the XFL from 2031 until 2034. He would then serve as the Assistant Offensive Coordinator / Quarterbacks Coach at Notre Dame from 2034 until 2044. Sexton is currently the Assistant Offensive Coordinator at his high school alma mater, the Central High School Raptors in Lansing, Michigan.

Scott Odom (2031-2049)

Main: Scott Odom

Scott Odom would take over as the head coach in 2031, making him the first former player to be hired as the head coach of the Red Devils. Odom had played quarterback for the Rosewood State Rosas, which is located in Union City. During his time at Rosewood State, Odom won a National Championship in his junior season, and the Heisman Trophy in his senior season. Odom also set multiple program records including the most passing yards in a single season, career passing yards, most rushing yards in a season, and most starts at quarterback in program history. On top of his Heisman, Odom won the Johnny Unitas Golden Arm award in his senior season, a Maxwell Award in his junior season, Walter Camp Award, and was named a first-team All-Mid-Atlantic Conference player in his junior and senior seasons. He was then drafted by the Baltimore Ravens, where he was the starting quarterback from 2007 until 2019. Following his retirement as a player, Odom would take a position only a year after his retirement as the Quarterbacks Coach for the Rosas.
