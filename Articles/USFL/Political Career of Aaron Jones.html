This article is about Aaron Jones' political career, for more information on Jones, see: Aaron Jones

Aaron Jones served as a member of the United States House of Representatives for Florida's 18th district. He had previously worked with the campaign for Helena Elizondo, and was a Conservative activist. Jones ran for the 18th Congressional District, which had been vacated due to the resignation of long-time Congressman Elijah Tucker. Jones was considered the favorite to win the seat due to his close connections with popular politician Helena Elizondo, as well as their similar ideologies. Jones would win the seat fairly comfortably, and would joined the America First Caucus upon his entrance into Congress. He also ran failed campaign for Governor of Florida. Jones supported many conservative bills including defunding planned parenthood, cuts to social security, loosened regulations on corporations, and tax cuts for the richest 1%. Jones was the front-runner for the majority of the race due to the unpopularity of incumbent Governor Frank Myers. After his failed gubernatorial campaign, Jones ran for his seat the next year in 2051, and won it back, and would hold onto the seat until his death in 2064. Jones was a member of the Republican Party. Jones served as the Chairman of controverisal right-wing America First Caucus from 2053 until 2063. In total, Jones served 10 terms in the House.

2032 Republican National Convention Speech

Marijuana

Jones spoke to the public on multiple occasions on his usage of marijuana for pain relief during his career as a football player. Jones was an advocate for the legalization of marijuana for medical and recreational usage. He also advocated for the removal of marijuana from the NFL's, and other leagues' banned substances list.

Concussions

Jones was also a vocal propnent of ensuring that football was safe, especially for children playing pee-wee and pop warner football. Jones became a vocal critic of the NFL's concussion protocols in comparsion to the ones implemented by the USFL. Saying that the "NFL is still trying to cover up the skeletons of CTE from the past." Jones also said that he was doing this in order to ensure the safety of players physical and mental health, and that he didn't want the league to be "soft." Jones also funded a $12 million dollar study on the effects of CTE on player's physical and mental health after leaving professional and college football.

Major Legislation

American Patriotic Black History Act

Main article: American Patriotic Black History Act

The American Patriotic Black History Act is a law passed by the 120th Congress on October 13, 2049. The act established guidelines regarding what school curricula can say regarding issues of racism, police brutality, and systemtic racism. The bill passed along party lines, and was signed into law by President Helena Elizondo. The law has been modified several times, howevever it has endured many attempts by Labour and Democratic Representatives to be completely repealed.

2050 Florida Gubernatorial Election

Jones ran an unsuccessful campaign for Governor of Florida following the retirement of Helena Elizondo. Initially polls indicated a substanial lead for Jones due in part to his close ties with Elizondo, who had an overall approval rating of 54%, and 96% approval rating amongst Republicans. Jones would fall behind in the polls in May of the 2050 due to a gaff during a speech in Orlando. Jones' lead would slip further following an ad run by Ethan Drake's campaign, painting Jones as rich and out of touch. Which would be further proven when Jones unveiled his tax plan, revealing deep tax cuts for the rich and de-regulation. He would fall behind fellow Elizondo disciple Myles Chu and would not be able to regain the lead.

Jones would attempt to regain the momentum lost by placing ads on TV describing Jones' childhood living on food stamps in Miami Gardens. This would work slightly, and placed Jones and Drake in a deadlock for second place in the polls. However, both remained at least 5 points behind Chu. The Jones campaign would launch a disastrous ad in October that attemtped to call into question Chu's American citizenship. This ad would be pulled two weeks later after intense backlash. The campaign would release a statement, and the campaign fell into 4th place in the polls. Jones performed decently well in the debate, answering questions from the audience. He would do an interview with Conservative radio host Jackson Adams, and was endorsed by the NRA and the National Conservative Union, and the Black Conservative Alliance.

Jones would come in third place during the primary election, and would drop out of the race a day later. Jones obtained a majority of the African-American vote, as well as a large portion of the Hispanic vote. Jones would however fail to moblize the core base of white suburban Republicans, which were essential for electing Elizondo as President and as Governor.

Abortion
