The 2020 seaon was the inaugural season for the Union City Red Devils, and the United States Football League. A draft occurred on May 10th, 2019 in order to determine the roster for the season, the draft consisted of 20 rounds over the span of 3 days. The team went 2-6, and finished 3rd in the Eastern Conference. The team was coached by Rick Hamilton.

Inaugural Draft

Main: USFL Draft

The inaurgural USFL Draft was held from May 10-13, 2019, which was the year before the beginning of the inaugural league season. The draft consisted of 42 rounds with each team being allocated one pick per round. Prior to the draft, the league designated specific colleges and universities local to each team in order to maintain a competitive balance and to promote local fan loyalty; however teams were not explicitly restricted from drafting a player from a school outisde of the assigned pipeline schools.

Teams were allowed to pick from current college juiors, and seniors during the draft, and were allowed to sign free agents from the NFL or other football leagues, or other players deemed eligible by the organization per league rules.

The league also created a salary cap for each team, with the maximum total salary being capped at $500 million dollars. Any team that was found to be exceeding the cap could face fines, loss of draft picks, or even suspension for a period of time proportionate to the violation.

    The first game in franchise history was controlled almost entirely by the Cincinnati Royals. The Royals would score on their second drive, and the Red Devils would go on to score their first touchdown in franchise history with a Aaron Jones to a John Oliver paass. The Royals controlled possesion for the majority of the game, and the Red Devils would go three and out on most of their drives in the first three quatrers. In the final quarter of regulation, the Red Devils went on a scoring spree, scoring on two of their four drives in the quarter. The Red Devils came back into the game, when they scored 10 unanswered points on the Royals, tying the game at 20 at the end of the fourth quarter.

    In overtime, the Royals won the coin toss. They would go on to score on a 10 play drive and the game ended with a game winning field goal by Patt Barkley.
