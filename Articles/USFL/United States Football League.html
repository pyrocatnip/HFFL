The United States Football League (USFL) is a professional American football league consisting of 32 teams split into two conferences. The league was founded in 2020, and is the second largest American football league in the United States after the NFL. The league currently utilizes a 17-week regular season schedule, with an addition four preseason games. The regular season typically runs from mid-February until May, with a single-elimination postseason from late-May into mid-June.

Corporate Structure

The USFL is the only professional American sports league that allows for ownership from the players, with players being given a vote for all major executive decision by the leaue. Players who have been in the league for longer are given greater say over the major decisions including but not limited to relocation, equipment supplier changes, and name changes.

Commissioner

Unlike with the NFL, where the Commissioner is somewhat beholden to the owners, the Commissioner has more executive control over each of the teams. The Commissioner of the USFL is the principal executive of the league, and has the authority to issue fines and penalties for various infractions. The Commissioner is directly elected by the Players' Association and served a term of 6 teams with no term limits. The Commissioner also has the authority to negotiate media contractts and hire executive employees, who are employed directly by the league.

Season Structure

Regular Season

Current Formula

The current USFL formula for the regular season is the same formatting used by the NFL. The schedule is 17 weeks in total, with each team playing 16 games, and being given one bye week. Following critera are used in order to determine the schedule for each team:

  • Each team plays twice against each of the other three teams in its division: once at home, and once on the road (six games).
  • Each team plays once against each of the four teams from a predetermined division (based on a three-year rotation) within its own conference: two at home, and two on the road (four games).
  • Each team plays once against each team from the remaining two divisions within its conference that finished in a similar placement in the final divisional standings in the prior season one at home, one on the road (two games)
  • Each team plays once against each of the four teams from a predetermined division (based on a four-year rotation) in the other conference: two at home, and two on the road (four games).

Past Formulas

From the 2020 season until the expansion in 2028, the schedule for the regular season simply involed each team playing each other two times per season, and being given an addition week for rest. The beginning of the regular season was the middle of February with the end of the regular season coming at the end of May.

Following the expansion of the league from 8 teams to 24, the league schedule changed in order to accomodate the newly created divisions. The league split the 24 teams into two conference of 12 with 6 teams in each division.
