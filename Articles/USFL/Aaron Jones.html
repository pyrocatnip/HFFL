Aaron Robert Kennedy Jones (May 8, 1999 – August 20, 2064) was a former American football, and a member of US Men's National gridiron football team. He played the majority of his professional career with the Union City Red Devils. He was drafted with the third overall pick in the inaugural USFL draft. He played for the Red Devils from 2020 until 2028. Jones was cut from the Red Devils following an 0-6, where he threw for 1,419 yards, 3 touchdowns and 9 interceptions. Jones would then play for the Sarasota Seamen, when they joined the USFL as an expansion franchise in 2031, and would start for the Seaman until the team folded in 2034.

Jones also was a member of the US men's national team for gridiron football, and participated in the first Summer Oympics to include the sport. He also partcipated in the World Girdiron Championship, and the North American Gridiron Championship. Jones would win his only gold medal in the 2020 games. After his retirement, Jones would become and activist for the legalization of marijuana. Jones passed away on August 20, 2064 from complications during his battle with lung cancer.

Early Life

Aaron Robert Kennedy Jones was born on May 8th, 1999 to Theresa and Michael Jones. Jones has two younger sisters, born in 2002 and 2004, and a half-brother on his mother's side. He grew up in a working class neighborhood of Miami Gardens, Florida. He would attend Vincent A. Jackson High School, and was a member of the varsity baseball team and the varsity football team. In the summer between his freshamn-sophomore year, Jones would work as a ball boy for the Miami Gardens Racquet Club, and fell in love with tennis. Jones would choose to play varsity tennis during his sophomore season.

High School Career

Jones played in a total of 54 matches during his sophomore season. He had a record of 16-38. An injury he suffered during the offseason would cause Jones to switch back to football by his junior season. By the end of his high school tennis career, Jones hadn't won any single's titles, but had won the Miami Gardens High School Tennis Tournament in doubles.

Jones would make the varsity football team in his junior season, however he wouldn't have been named the starting quarterback due to the lack of film on him, and due to having not played football since pop warner. Instead Jones played Tight End for his junior season in order to get playing time for college scouts. Jones record a total of 59 blocks, had 0 sacks allowed, 59 receptions for 347 yards, and 2 touchdowns. Jones remained a tight end going into his senior season. He would have an excellent season with 108 receptions, 1,451 yards, and 20 touchdowns. However, he was not receiving a lot of offers from D1 programs, and continuously tried to play quarterback, as he believed he would have a better chance at landing a D1 scholarship at Quarterback.

Recruiting

Jones was recruited as a tight end, and was considered a three star athlete according to most analysts. However certain outlets such as Rivals, and ESPN would rate him as a two star athlete. Jones received offers from D1 schools like Tennessee, Kansas, Utah, Vanderbilt, as well as from D2 schools including the Bowie State University, University of Charleston, University of Minnesota, Duluth, Winston-Salem State University, and Virginia State University. The only one who offered him a scholarship at quarterback being Bowie State.

College Career

In his freshman season at Bowie State, Jones would play in 6 games over 13 starts. He would complete 89 of 109 passes, threw for 1,379 yards, 8 touchdowns and 12 interceptions. He was primarily utilized as a Run-pass option or RPO quarterback. Jones won 3 of his 6 games, and was named MVP for his performance in week 13, where he completed 76% of his passes and had 4 passing touchdowns and 1 rushing touchdown. Jones sat out from his sophomore season due to injury.

Professional Career

Union Red Devils (2020-2028)

Jones was drafted 3rd overall in the inaugural USFL draft in 2020 by the Union City Red Devils, and was the starting quarterback for the first nine seasons. Jones would sign a 5 year contract worth $18.6 million dollars, which was extended following a solid 2024 campaign in which he led the USFL in touchdowns with 37. In his nine year career in the USFL, Jones failed to post a single 4,00+ yard passing season, and threw for more interceptions than touchdowns. Jones' career at Union City ended in 2028 following an 0-6 start. He would be released into free agency in the offseason, and did receive interest in workouts with multiple teams.

2020

Main: 2020 Union City Red Devils season

Jones' first professional football game saw him lose 20-23 in overtime. Jones threw for 178 yards, and had two touchdowns and one interception. Jones would also rush for an additional 50 yards on 3 attempts, and fumbled the ball once. A week later, Jones would throw 2 interceptions, one of which was a pick six returned for a touchdown. Jones only completed 38% of his passes and threw for only 102 yards. The Red Devils would lose on a last minute hail mary in the fourth quarter with a final score of 17-24. Week 3 would be the first win in franchise history for the Red Devils. Jones completed 21-33, threw of 258 yards with 2 touchdowns and an interception. He would also rush for 100 yards and a rushing touchdown winning the first home game in franchise history with a final scoe of 31-30. The Red Devils would not be able to take the momentum from their win in week 2 into week three, losing 10-33 to the Boston Minutemen at Boston University Field. Jones completed 10 of 28 passes, and threw four interceptions in the second half before being benched for Jarrod Payne.

Following a devestating loss in week 3, the Red Devils would loss in a close matchup against the Chicago Tornadoes in week 4. Jones was given the starting job, and was trying to prove himself as being worthy of the starting quarterback job. Unfortunately, Jones would have another poor performance, throwing another 2 interceptions and 1 touchdown. With the loss, the Red Devils fell to 1-4, and it was looking like they were not going to be contendors for the forseeable fututre. Week 6 was decent for Jones, throwing for 21 of 39 passes, 256 yards, 1 touchdowns and no interceptions. Coming out of the bye week, the pressure on Jones and the offense to perform well grew. Jones would rise to the occasion with the best performance in his career. He would lead the Red Devils on two scoring drives after the defense had let the Wildcats run all over them. Jones led the Red Devils to come back from 21 point deficit in the second quarter, and the Red Devils would have their second win of the season.

The rest of the season saw mixed play from Jones, and the Red Devils went 4-12 in their first season, and would pick third overall in the 2021 draft.

2028: End of an Era

Las Vegas Vultures (2028)

Jones signed a 1 year contract worth $1.6 million dollars in the week 15 of the 2028 season with the Las Vegas Vultures, who had previously started four quarterbacks in the 2028 season, and posted the worst record in the league at 2-14. He would eventually be cut in the preseason in favor of Oscar Vargas, who had previously been the backup quarterback during Jones' stint with the Red Devils.

First Retirement (2029-2030)

Jones remained a free agent during he 2029 season, and would be signed to the practice squad for the Seattle Dragons of the XFL, however, he failed to make the final roster. Jones was then signed to the practie squad for the Tampa Bay Mauraders, where he once again failed to make the final roster. Jones would retire at the end of the 2029 season after receiving no offers from any team during the 2029 season. During his retirement, Jones would become a football analyst for the USFL Sports Network, and also made apperances on ESPN, and Fox Sports. He would also tease an autobiography, which he had begun writing in 2026. Jones would only retire for two full seasons until he returned to the USFL in 2031 with the newly created Sarasota Seaman expansion franchise.

Sarasota Seaman (2031-2034)

Jones returned to the USFL as the starting quarterback for the Sarastoa Seamen, who joined the USFL as an expansion team in 2031. He would play with the Seamen until the team folded in 2034 following poor attendance and financial mismangaement by the front office.

Activism

Right to Life Rallies

Jones has participated in multiple right to life rallies, advocating for restrictions on abortions. Jones has also publicly advocated for defunding Planned Parenthood.

Helena Elizondo Campaign

Jones took to social media in supprot of Helena Elizondo for President of the United States. Jones would attend rallies in California for Elizondo, and would attend rallies in Los Angeles, San Francisco, and San Diego, in which he was a keynote speaker. He endoresed Elizondo as an outsider, who would favor small businesses, limited social spending, traditional Christian values, and ending what he called "indoctrination in schools." Jones also joined Elizondo's campaign in California in the hope that Jones' endorsement would make the historically Democratic stronghold flip for the first time. Jones would join the staff as a Senior Executive for the Elizondo Campaign in California. Under his leadership, the Elizndo camapaign would see a surge in support from minority communties in California, however, not enough to make the state competitive. Jones would instead focus on a nationwide camapgin, and was promoted into Elizondo's national Presidential campaign as an executive.

Under Jones, the Elizondo camapign launched ads attacking the platforms of Elizondo's opponents, including an ad depicting Republican Sentor Cory Gassley as being "soft on crime," and another add which very nearly called Elizondo's Roderick Kendall as a "communist". Jones' other priorities on the Elizondo campaign included trying to swing the votes of sububuran families, which had previously been a strong conservative base up until the last two elections cycles. In order to appeal to that demopgraphic, the Elizondo campaign focused on the anti-Critical Race Theory movement, as well as vaccines. However, the campaign also tried to distance itself from the far-right, and QAnon elements of those viewpoints. In addition, the campaign focused on issues such as School Choice, and preventing "un-ethical" literature in schools.

Marijuana

Jones spoke to the public on multiple occasions on his usage of marijuana for pain relief during his career as a football player. Jones was an advocate for the legalization of marijuana for medical and recreational usage. He also advocated for the removal of marijuana from the NFL's, and other leagues' banned substances list.

Concussions

Jones was also a vocal propnent of ensuring that football was safe, especially for children playing pee-wee and pop warner football. Jones became a vocal critic of the NFL's concussion protocols in comparsion to the ones implemented by the USFL. Saying that the "NFL is still trying to cover up the skeletons of CTE from the past." Jones also said that he was doing this in order to ensure the safety of players physical and mental health, and that he didn't want the league to be "soft." Jones also funded a $12 million dollar study on the effects of CTE on player's physical and mental health after leaving professional and college football.

Legal Issues

40Coin

Main: 40Coin

Jones was indicted on January 10th, 2029 of fraud related to the Cryptocurrency, 40Coin, of which he was a principle investor.

Tax Fraud

In 2036, the IRS investigated and brought up allegations of tax fraud and tax evasion, and racketeering on Jones, claiming he hadn't paid any taxes since the beginning of his professional football career, as well as illegal campaign dontations made on his behalf by a PAC run by Jones. The case was brought to the Southern District of California, and Jones was later found guilty of tax fraud and was sentenced of 38 months in federal prison, along with a fine of $11.2 million dollars, and 5 years of probation. Jones would challenge this, claiming that he was being falsely persecuted by what in his mind a "biased left-wing judge." The verdict was upheld three months later, and Jones served 3 months in federal prison.

Domestic Violence Allegations

In 2041, 18 women came forward with allegations of sexual assault, sexual harrassment, and stalking by Jones. Jones would initially deny these accusations, however he would eventually admit under oath that he had indeed stalked at least 3 women. Following this, a restraining order was issued on the accusing parties behalf, and Jones was once again placed on probation and pay another $12 million dollars in damages to the accusers. The 5 of the remaining cases would be dropped within a year of coming forward, while the other ten were settled out of court or ended with a plea bargain and no prison time. None of the accusers brought criminal charges against Jones, therefore discreditng their accusations in the eyes of many. The public reception to these allegations was mixed, with many men's rights activists, anti-feminist, and other right-wing organizations and figures voicing their support for Jones and believing he was falsely accused for publicity. Many other groups would vocally disavow Jones, including many of his former teammates as well as an Union City Red Devils spokesperson.

Divorce

On April 19th, 2041, Aisha Jones filed for divorce from Aaron Jones, following the revelation of domestic violence allegations from 18 women. Aisha also claimed that Aaron had threatened her on multiple occasions and was "generally violent" towards their children. As a part of the divorce proceedings, Jones was ordered to pay child support, and was not allowed to be in contact with his children or ex-wife without supervision. Jones spoke out following this case, calling the case another example of the second-class treatment that fathers receive.

Political Views

Jones was a registered Democrat from 2016 until 2025. He would reigster as a Republican in 2025, due to his belief that the Democrats are failing the African-American community. He said he regretted voting for Roderick Kendall in the 2024 election. Jones posted on Twitter on May 21st, 2025, "The adminsitration has failed to deliver on the promises made to the Black community, once again failing to meet the needs of the community who got him and the other Democrats in offce;" this post was in reference to the continued water crisis in Tuskegee, Alabama. Jones became a very outspoken Conservative, and a vocal supporter of Helena Elizondo in her 2024, 2028, and 2032 Presidential camapigns, as well as Elizondo's campaign for re-election as Governor of Florida. He has publicly endorsed right-wing candidates in various elections inclduing controversial right-wing commentator Matthew Paul for Governor of California. He has been also been critical of the Black Lives Matter movement, calling it a "terorist organization of far-left radicalism", and that "the [Black Lives Matter] movement is doing more harm than good for the African-American community."

Abortion

Jones was a vocally pro-life, believing that abortion was "murdering children." He spoke out in favor of criminal punishments for women seeking abortions, as in his opinion, they were guilty of murder.

Murder of La'Sean Johnson

Main: Murder of La'Sean Johnson

Jones came under fire by many in the African-American community after going on Fox News, and falsely claiming that "La'Sean Johnson wouldn't have died, if he hadn't been running from the police." Jones added that the radical-left are going to use the murder of another black man to further their radical agenda of defunding the police. Jones would also change his Twitter background to an image with the words "All Lives Matter," which is a common retort to "Black Lives Matter." These actions led to the cancellation of multiple events that Jones was planned to attend. Jones would state that this was another example of the left "trying to keep people in line".

Induction into the Trailblazers Club

Main: Trailblazers Club

As Jones was the first quarterback taken in the inaugural USFL draft, and thus a member of the first year of the USFL's history, Aaron Jones was planned to be honored by being inducted in to the Trailblazers Club, along with all the other 2020 USFL draftees. However, due to widespread backlash and protests against an induction, the USFL decided not to induct Jones in 2042. The League Office would reconsider Jones for induction in 2049, with a referndum being held for all members of the USFL to vote on. Overwhelmingly, the electorate voted against inducting Jones into the club of pioneers of the league.
