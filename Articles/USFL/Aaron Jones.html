Aaron Robert Kennedy Jones (May 8, 1999 – August 20, 2064) was a former Congressman for Florida's 18th Congressional District, and a former professional American football, and a member of US Men's National gridiron football team. He played the majority of his professional career with the Union City Red Devils. He was drafted with the third overall pick in the inaugural USFL draft. He played for the Red Devils from 2020 until 2028. Jones was cut from the Red Devils following an 0-6, where he threw for 1,419 yards, 3 touchdowns and 9 interceptions. Jones would then play for the Sarasota Seamen, when they joined the USFL as an expansion franchise in 2031, and would start for the Seaman until the team folded in 2034. Jones was the only starting quarterback from the original 2020 USFL Draft to never win a National Championship, National Championship MVP, leage MVP, first team All-Pro, or quarterback of the year award. He was also only one of two starting quarterbacks who never had a 4,500+ passing yard season, and the only one to throw for over 20 interceptions in a single season.

Jones also was a member of the US men's national team for gridiron football, and participated in the first Summer Oympics to include the sport. He also partcipated in the World Girdiron Championship, and the North American Gridiron Championship. Jones would win his only gold medal in the 2020 games. After his retirement, Jones would become and activist for the legalization of marijuana. He was also very vocal on his conservative views. After retiring from professional football, Jones entered politics. He ran for the vacant seat for Florida's 18th Congressional District in 2045, and held office until his death in 2064. Jones passed away on August 20, 2064 from complications during his battle with lung cancer.

Early Life

Aaron Robert Kennedy Jones was born on May 8th, 1999 to Theresa and Michael Jones. Jones has two younger sisters, born in 2002 and 2004, and a half-brother on his mother's side. He grew up in a working class neighborhood of Miami Gardens, Florida. He would attend Liberty High School for the first half of his freshman year, however Jones would transfer after getting into a physical altercation with a member of the school security staff. Jones allgedly hit the guard who was trying to break up an altercation between Jones and another student that was the result of a rumor that his girlfriend was cheating on him. Jones transferred to Vincent A. Jackson High School, and was a member of the varsity baseball team and the varsity football team. In the summer between his freshamn-sophomore year, Jones would work as a ball boy for the Miami Gardens Racquet Club, and fell in love with tennis. Jones would choose to play varsity tennis during his sophomore season.

High School Career

Jones played in a total of 54 matches during his sophomore season. He had a record of 16-38. An injury he suffered during the offseason would cause Jones to switch back to football by his junior season. By the end of his high school tennis career, Jones hadn't won any single's titles, but had won the Miami Gardens High School Tennis Tournament in doubles. Jones would make the varsity football team in his junior season, however he wouldn't have been named the starting quarterback due to the lack of film on him, and due to having not played football since pop warner. Instead Jones played Tight End for his junior season in order to get playing time for college scouts. Jones record a total of 59 blocks, had 0 sacks allowed, 59 receptions for 347 yards, and 2 touchdowns. Jones remained a tight end going into his senior season. He would have an excellent season with 108 receptions, 1,451 yards, and 20 touchdowns. However, he was not receiving a lot of offers from D1 programs, and continuously tried to play quarterback, as he believed he would have a better chance at landing a D1 scholarship at Quarterback.

Jones was suspended for the entire senior season after being found in possesion of marijuana in the locker room. The school did not report this to the police as they considered his lack of playtime a suitable punishment.

Recruiting

Jones was recruited as a tight end, and was considered a three star athlete according to most analysts. However certain outlets such as Rivals, and ESPN would rate him as a two star athlete. Jones received offers from D1 schools like Tennessee, Kansas, Utah, Vanderbilt, as well as from D2 schools including the Bowie State University, University of Charleston, University of Minnesota, Duluth, Winston-Salem State University, and Virginia State University. Jones would visit Auburn, and Bowie State, however Bowie State was the only school to offer Jones a scholarship. Jones committed to Bowie State as a three star tight end, and was ranked as #561 in the country, and #38 of all the tight ends in the freshman class.

College Career

Jones would commit to Bowie State University, a historically black university located in Bowie, Maryland on May 21st, 2015. In his freshman season at Bowie State, Jones would play in 6 games over 13 starts. He would complete 89 of 109 passes, threw for 1,379 yards, 8 touchdowns and 12 interceptions. He was primarily utilized as a Run-pass option or RPO quarterback. Jones won 3 of his 6 games, and was named MVP for his performance in week 13, where he completed 76% of his passes and had 4 passing touchdowns and 1 rushing touchdown. Jones sat out from his sophomore season due to injury. Jones would see the field again in his junior season. He would lead Bowie State to an FCS Bowl Game, where he won the Player of the Game Award. He threw for 301 yards, 5 touchdowns, and 2 intercpetions, completed 56% of his passes, and had 129 yards rushing. Jones would decide to remain in college for a final season, however he would be suspended by the NCAA for allegedly signing autographs in the offseason. As a result of this, most scouts listed Jones as a serious liability, and his draft stock completely pulmmeted. He would only receieve an interview with the Browns.

Jones would then announce on February 10th, 2020 that he would be joining the newly formed United States Football Legaue. Jones entered the inaugural USFL draft as a potential boom or bust pick. He would evenetually be drafted by the Union City Red Devils 3rd overall. Jones would never receieve an offer from another NFL franchise for the remainder of his professional career, however he would become a decent to above average starting quarterback in the USFL.

Union Red Devils (2020-2028)

Jones was drafted 3rd overall in the inaugural USFL draft in 2020 by the Union City Red Devils, and was the starting quarterback for the first nine seasons. Jones would sign a 5 year contract worth $18.6 million dollars, which was extended following a solid 2024 campaign in which he led the USFL in touchdowns with 37. In his nine year career in the USFL, Jones failed to post a single 4,00+ yard passing season, and threw for more interceptions than touchdowns. Jones' career at Union City ended in 2028 following an 0-6 start. He would be released into free agency in the offseason, and did receive interest in workouts with multiple teams.

2020

Main article: 2020 Union City Red Devils season

Jones' first professional football game saw him lose 20-23 in overtime. Jones threw for 178 yards, and had two touchdowns and one interception. Jones would also rush for an additional 50 yards on 3 attempts, and fumbled the ball once. A week later, Jones would throw 2 interceptions, one of which was a pick six returned for a touchdown. Jones only completed 38% of his passes and threw for only 102 yards. The Red Devils would lose on a last minute hail mary in the fourth quarter with a final score of 17-24. Week 3 would be the first win in franchise history for the Red Devils. Jones completed 21-33, threw of 258 yards with 2 touchdowns and an interception. He would also rush for 100 yards and a rushing touchdown winning the first home game in franchise history with a final scoe of 31-30. The Red Devils would not be able to take the momentum from their win in week 2 into week three, losing 10-33 to the Boston Minutemen at Boston University Field. Jones completed 10 of 28 passes, and threw four interceptions in the second half before being benched for Jarrod Payne.

Following a devestating loss in week 3, the Red Devils would loss in a close matchup against the Chicago Tornadoes in week 4. Jones was given the starting job, and was trying to prove himself as being worthy of the starting quarterback job. Unfortunately, Jones would have another poor performance, throwing another 2 interceptions and 1 touchdown. With the loss, the Red Devils fell to 1-4, and it was looking like they were not going to be contendors for the forseeable fututre. Week 6 was decent for Jones, throwing for 21 of 39 passes, 256 yards, 1 touchdowns and no interceptions. Coming out of the bye week, the pressure on Jones and the offense to perform well grew. Jones would rise to the occasion with the best performance in his career. He would lead the Red Devils on two scoring drives after the defense had let the Wildcats run all over them. Jones led the Red Devils to come back from 21 point deficit in the second quarter, and the Red Devils would have their second win of the season.

2021

Main article: 2021 Union City Red Devils season

Jones' second season in the USFL, saw Jones take a step back statistically. The Red Devils improved to 6-10, however they would still finish 3rd in the Eastern Conference. Jones would throw 26 interceptions, which was the most thrown in a single season at the time. Aaron Jones would throw for under 3,000 yards, 18 touchdowns, and 26 interceptions. In week 8, Jones would throw for 130 yards, 2 touchdowns and 5 interceptions. He would complete only 52% of his passes and had only 4.3 yards per average. In a week 10 game against the Chicago Tornadoes, Jones had a completion percentage of 59% of his passes and would rush for 219 yards and 3 touchdowns on the ground. However, in the passing game, Jones would continue to struggle and had 1 touchdown and 3 interceptions. Beginning in week 15 of the season Jones would resfuse to take the field due to a contract dispute with the Red Devils. Blake Rosier would take over the starting role, then veteran QB Jeffrey Cox, would finish out the season with the Red Devils going 6-10 and once again failing to make the National Championship.

2022

Main article: 2022 Union City Red Devils season

Jones continued his contract holdout in the 2022 season, and would not take the field for the entire season. Jones asked for a long-term deal with $108 million in guaranteed money. The Red Devils were not willing to take any deal with a majority of the salary being guaranteed. Instead of playing with the Red Devils, Jones participated in the World Gridiron Championship. In week 4 of the regular season, contract negoatiations began with the Red Devils offering $78 million dollars over 4 years, with 32 million guaranteed. Jones would publicly announce his rejection of the offering on a Twitter post. He would then post on social media pictures of himself in himself in other teams' jerseys, sparking rumors that he would be asking to be traded. In week 6, the Red Devils would offer Jones $99 million over 5 years with $27 million guaranteed. Jones would once again reject this offer, and would ask to be traded. This offer was rejected by the team, and head coach Rick Hamilton would say in a press confeence that he hoped that Jones would return to the Red Devils.

In the 13th week of the regular season, the Red Devils would give Jones an ultimatum of either accepting the team's offer, or Jones would be given a franchise tag, and would have to remain with the team util at least the end of the 2023 season. The Red Devils also floated the idea of a Designated Player Tag after, keeping Jones in Union City until the age of 26. Jones again declined the offer by the Red Devils, and the team would franchise tag Jones. In protest, Jones refused to even attend practice and any team meetings until he was given a long-term contract with all or a majority guaranteed money. Jones was allegedly in talks with the Boston Minutemen, who were offering him $79 million guaranteed over 5 years. After the story broke the Red Devils fined Jones for going and speaking to other teams without permission. He was fined $150,000, and was suspended for 10 games as soon as a contract was set. Jones would unfollow the Red Devils on all social media accounts, and removed all mentions of the Red Devils from his social media accounts. This would be followed by rumors in the sports media that Jones was going to sign with the Alabama Rocketeers. The following day, April 18, 2022, Jones would be recorded speaking poorly of his teammates. He allegedly called head coach Rick Hamilton a "moron," and was subsequently fined another $85,000 and was suspended for an additional 4 games. Jones was placed on the Disciplinary Reserve list, which was the first time that this was used by a team in the USFL.

Jones' team would issue an apology on social media to the Red Devils for his conduct the following week. Jones was seen in Union City for the first time since the 2021 season, where he attneded a charity drive run by the Red Devils for children facing poverty. He would attend a meeting with the owner of the Red Devils the next day, and Jones would announce that he would indeed be remaining in Union City for the forseeabel future, however at the time no formal offer had been given to Jones. The Red Devils receieved an offer from the Minutemen to trade Jones for a first round pick, and two second round picks; however the offer would be rejected the day after. Jones called a press conference on June 18th stating that he was in talks with the front office of the Red Devils, and it was all but certain that he'd remain with the team. He would also apologize for his actions, and the Red Devils would waive their second suspension of 8 games as a result, however the 10 game suspension remained in place. He accepted his fine, acknowledging his mistakes and vowing to do better in the future.

2023

Main article: 2023 Union City Red Devils season

Jones would sit out the majority of the 2023 season due to the team's 10 game suspension. In the week 4 bye week, Jones would accpet a 4 year, $101 million dollar contract with $36.5 million guaranteed. Jones would be the named the starter in week 11 of the season, which was his first game since week 14 of the 2020 season. His first game back was disaster with 5 interceptions thrown in the first half. He would complete 34% of his passes, and threw for only 168 yards. In the following week Jones would throw for 288 yards, 3 touchdowns and 2 interceptions in a 44-21 loss to the . Jones was able to pass for 1,501 yards and threw 10 touchdowns and 5 interceptions. The Red Devils would go 8-8 and placed 2nd in the Eastern Conference. The season marked the first non-losing record in Red Devils history. The Red Devils were expected to be competitive in the next season, as the Red Devils acquired a ample draft capital and had begun to assemble a young core of players. The Red Devils once again failed to make the postseason, and failed to win a Conference Championship. He would have his best career game in the week 13 match against the New York Sentinels. He thew for 389 yards, 6 touchdowns, 2 interceptions, and he rushed for an additional 237 yards, and had 3 touchdowns on the ground. At the end of the season, questions still lingered on whether or not Jones would be the future of the Red Devils.

Jones would show flashes of elite talent in week 16. He threw for 265 yards, 4 touchdowns, and 1 intercpetion. He would also have a fumble taken 59 yards for a touchdown. The game was lost to the Orlando Sun with the Sun driving down the field on their first possession in overtime.

Activism

Right to Life Rallies

Jones has participated in multiple right to life rallies, advocating for restrictions on abortions. Jones has also publicly advocated for defunding Planned Parenthood.

Helena Elizondo Campaign

Jones took to social media in supprot of Helena Elizondo for President of the United States. Jones would attend rallies in California for Elizondo, and would attend rallies in Los Angeles, San Francisco, and San Diego, in which he was a keynote speaker. He endoresed Elizondo as an outsider, who would favor small businesses, limited social spending, traditional Christian values, and ending what he called "indoctrination in schools." Jones also joined Elizondo's campaign in California in the hope that Jones' endorsement would make the historically Democratic stronghold flip for the first time. Jones would join the staff as a Senior Executive for the Elizondo Campaign in California. Under his leadership, the Elizndo camapaign would see a surge in support from minority communties in California, however, not enough to make the state competitive. Jones would instead focus on a nationwide camapgin, and was promoted into Elizondo's national Presidential campaign as an executive.

Under Jones, the Elizondo camapign launched ads attacking the platforms of Elizondo's opponents, including an ad depicting Republican Sentor Cory Gassley as being "soft on crime," and another add which very nearly called Elizondo's Roderick Kendall as a "communist". Jones' other priorities on the Elizondo campaign included trying to swing the votes of sububuran families, which had previously been a strong conservative base up until the last two elections cycles. In order to appeal to that demopgraphic, the Elizondo campaign focused on the anti-Critical Race Theory movement, as well as vaccines. However, the campaign also tried to distance itself from the far-right, and QAnon elements of those viewpoints. In addition, the campaign focused on issues such as School Choice, and preventing "un-ethical" literature in schools.

Political Career

Main articles: Political Career of Aaron Jones, and 2045 United States House of Representatives Special Election. For more information on his political beliefs, see Political Positions of Aaron Jones

Aaron Jones successfully ran for the opened House of Representatives' seat for Florida's 18th District, which had been held by Democrat Patt Rogers from 1986 until 2004, and then by Elijah Tucker since 2005. The district consisted mostly of suburbs nearby Fort Lauderdale. Jones was able to safely win the seat in a special election following the resignation of Elijah Tucker. In the primary, Jones faced long-time Helena Elizondo critic Laura Ortiz. Jones was able to win in the primary by a margin of 6.54%. In the general, Jones was expected to lose due to the long-time control that the Democratic party had over the district. Instead, Jones was able to defeat Democratic challenger Doug Naples, thanks to in-roads made into the African-American, Latinx, and other communities of color. Jones served as in Congress as a member of the Republican party, where his vote was aligned with that of the Congressional Republican party 87% of the time. The only times that Jones broke from the party line was on bills that would legalize/decriminalize the possession and usage of Marijuana, and voting for the appointment of Supreme Court justice Melinda Shields, a long time member of the Democratic party, and ally to the Blue Dog Democrats.

Jones ran an unsuccessful campaign for Governor of Florida in 2050, losing in the primaries to former Attorney General, Myles Chu.

Legal Issues

40Coin

Main: 40Coin

Jones was indicted on January 10th, 2029 of fraud related to the Cryptocurrency, 40Coin, of which he was a principle investor.

Tax Fraud

In 2036, the IRS investigated and brought up allegations of tax fraud and tax evasion, and racketeering on Jones, claiming he hadn't paid any taxes since the beginning of his professional football career, as well as illegal campaign dontations made on his behalf by a PAC run by Jones. The case was brought to the Southern District of California, and Jones was later found guilty of tax fraud and was sentenced of 38 months in federal prison, along with a fine of $11.2 million dollars, and 5 years of probation. Jones would challenge this, claiming that he was being falsely persecuted by what in his mind a "biased left-wing judge." The verdict was upheld three months later, and Jones served 3 months in federal prison.

Domestic Violence Allegations

In 2041, 18 women came forward with allegations of sexual assault, sexual harrassment, and stalking by Jones. Jones would initially deny these accusations, however he would eventually admit under oath that he had indeed stalked at least 3 women. Following this, a restraining order was issued on the accusing parties behalf, and Jones was once again placed on probation and pay another $12 million dollars in damages to the accusers. The 5 of the remaining cases would be dropped within a year of coming forward, while the other ten were settled out of court or ended with a plea bargain and no prison time. None of the accusers brought criminal charges against Jones, therefore discreditng their accusations in the eyes of many. The public reception to these allegations was mixed, with many men's rights activists, anti-feminist, and other right-wing organizations and figures voicing their support for Jones and believing he was falsely accused for publicity. Many other groups would vocally disavow Jones, including many of his former teammates as well as an Union City Red Devils spokesperson.

Divorce

On April 18th, 2041, Aisha Jones filed for divorce from Aaron Jones, following the revelation of domestic violence allegations from 18 women. Aisha also claimed that Aaron had threatened her on multiple occasions and was "generally violent" towards their children. As a part of the divorce proceedings, Jones was ordered to pay child support, and was not allowed to be in contact with his children or ex-wife without supervision. Jones spoke out following this case, calling the case another example of the second-class treatment that fathers receive.

Death

On March 18, 2063, Jones undertook treatment at St. John's Hospital in Miami, Florida. Jones would eventually be diagnosed with stage IV lung cancer. He would pass away on August 20, 2064 of pneumonia at the age of 65. Following the news of his death, many prominent conservative members of Congress sent their condolences to the family. Democratic House, Doug Martindale also spoke with the family of Jones in the days after the passing of Aaron Jones. Jones' former head coach Rick Hamilton also epxressed his sadness at the loss of Jones.

Induction into the Trailblazers Club

Main: Trailblazers Club

As Jones was the first quarterback taken in the inaugural USFL draft, and thus a member of the first year of the USFL's history, Aaron Jones was planned to be honored by being inducted in to the Trailblazers Club, along with all the other 2020 USFL draftees. However, due to widespread backlash and protests against an induction, the USFL decided not to induct Jones in 2042. The League Office would reconsider Jones for induction in 2049, with a referndum being held for all members of the USFL to vote on. Overwhelmingly, the electorate voted against inducting Jones into the club of pioneers of the league.
