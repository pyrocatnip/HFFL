The 2020 season was Prather's first season as a professional tennis player. She began the year ranked #33 in the world according to the WTA Rankings. She won her first professional tournament in January, when she defeated Willow Long 6–4, 6–2 in the Los Angeles Futures. She would not see deep tournament runs during the month of februrary, with eliminations in the firt 3 rounds of play in every tournament she played in the month. Prather broke the losing streak in March, winning the Miami Open, her first Masters 1000 tournament run, and title.

Prater is currently ranked No. 7 in the WTA rankings.

Year Summary

Early hard court season

Los Angeles Futures

Prather in her first professional tournament won the Los Angeles Futures tournament with ease. She would win on straight sets in every one of her matches, and won two consecutive games without conceeding a single set. She would win the final game of the tournament 6–4, 6–2 winning her first career title.

Mexico City Futures

Prather lost in the first round of the Mexico City Futures, her first doubles tournament. She lost to the eventual champions Harris and Yoo, 7–6, 2–6, 6‐7.

Palo Alto Open

Palo Alto was Prather's first Challenger Tour event. She would make it to the semifinals, where she lost to fellow American Elizabeth Hauser in straight sets, 4–6, 5–7.

New York Open

Prather would make it to the round of 16, where she would lose to No. 8 ranked Sarah Chung 3–6, 4–6.

Dubai-Emirates Open

Prather had a second consecutive round of 16 exit in the Dubai-Emirates Open the following week. She lost to No. 13 Emma Radacanu 6–4, 4–6, 6–7.

Indian Wells Futures

Prather was eliminated in the first round of the Indian Wells Futures tournament, losing 3–6, 6–2, 5–3.

Miami Open

In Miami, Prather won her first major tournament. She would win convincingly against Dominica Ladas in the first round 6–2, 6–2, then she would follow that up with an equally dominant win over Margaret Kraus in the second round. She would then battle it out against Maria Sharapova, which was her third match against a top 10 opponent. She would escape with a narrow victory, with the final set coming down to a 10–2 tiebreaker. Prather would once again struggle against No. 23 Kristina Mladenovic with the final score of 1–6, 6–3, 6–0. In her first quarterfinals match in a major tournament, Prather would win against Karolina Pliskova 6–4, 6–4. Two weeks later, she would face Emma Radacanu in the finals for a rematch of her game in the round of 16 of the Dubai-Emirates. Prather would win this time, with a final of 4–6, 6–3, 7–5 sealing her first major title.

Marrakesh Open

Prather would have a good showing in the Marrakesh Open the following week. She would make it to the semifinals before losing to No. 1 ranked Hyojin Choi in straight sets. The final score of the match was: 3–6, 4–6.

Clay and grass court season

Banco Santander Open

Prather lost in the first round of the Banco Santander Open to Angela Silvestri 4–6, 6–3, 6–7.

Madrid Open

In the first round of the Madrid Open Prather upset Ariel Blackford and completely dominated the match. Prather had 0 unforced errors and did not lose a single set winning 6–0, 6–0. Blackford would come close to winning a game, however she would quickly fall behind and was unable to recover. In the second round, Prather beat Lillie Thomas in straight sets 6–1, 6–0. For a second consecutive game she would have no double faults or unforced errors. Against Maria Sharapova in the third round, Prather would have a cold streak through the first 3/4ths of the set. Sharapova capitalized on this going winning 5 games to 2. However, Prather would reverse her cold streak and win 3 straight games, tying the first set at 5 each. This included two straight games where Prather didn't allow Sharapova to score a single point. Prather would ultimately win the set 7–6 (10–2)

Italian Open

Prather faced Serena Williams in the fourth round, where she would scrape a win out 7–5, 6–4, once again upsetting the favorite to win the title coming into the tournment. This would be only the second time that Prather had defeated a top ranked opponent. And the first time she beat a top ranked opponent in straight sets.
