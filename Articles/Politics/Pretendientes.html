Pretendientes

A Pejorative label for Conservatives in favor of Puerto Rican statehood

Pretendientes is a pejorative label for members of the Conservative-Republican Party who were favor of Puerto Rican statehood. The term originated during the 2046 Puerto Rican statehood referendum campaign. It was used to differentiate between those who were in favor of statehood and who were against statehood. The term was often alluded to in attack ads during the campaign, labelling them as "false Conservatives," or conservatives in name only (CINO).

The term was most often used against Hispanic, specifically Puerto Rican Conservatives in favor of statehood. While those who use thed the term denied any racial connotation, many still believed that there was a racial component to the attacks.

Origin

The term pretendientes is short for los conservadores pretendients, which translates to pretend conservative in English. The term was first seen in a billboard near San Juan during the lead up to the first round referendum in 2046. The billboard said:

"No dejes que tu destino sea dictado por Washington y los conservadores pretendientes.
Don't let your fate be determined by Washington and the pretender conservatives
vote por el status quo"
vote for the status quo

The term "los conservadores pretendientes" quickly gained popularity in conservative pro-status quo circles. The term shot up to #2 in trending for Twitter in Puerto Rican for 2 days after the billboard first went viral on social media. The phrase "pretendientes" became popular on TikTok and was rapidly gaining grounds as an ad hominem attack on pro-Statehood conservatives. Pro-statehood politicans were then quickly were recieving hundreds of comments on even un-related posts calling them "pretendientes."

Usage

The term pretendientes was never directly used by official pro-status quo campaigns, however it was alluded to during many speeches, as well as used by unofficial campaigns such as the Encuentro Party and the Together for Puerto Rico. Many Catholic radicals also used the term in opposition to their catholic minority in the United States and anti-Catholic sentiments in the United States.

People labelled as Pretendientes

The following is a list of people who have been labelled as pretendientes:

  • Adam Sawyer – Former U.S. Ambassador to Cuba
  • Saul Gurerro – Governor of Puerto Rico
  • Laura Montez – Puerto Rican Senator
  • Cecilia Ramirez – U.S. Representative from Florida's 4th District
  • Joe Salvini – Former U.S. Senator from Florida
