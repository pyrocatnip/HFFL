Florida's 18th Congressional District is an electoral district for the U.S. Congress, located in southeastern Florida. The district includes Port St. Lucie, Fort Pierce. Stuart, and Juniper. The district also inlcudes Treasure Coast International Airport.

Since 2012, the district contains the whole of St. Luice County, and Martin County, as well as the northeastern part of Palm Beach County.

The district is currently represented by member of the America First Party Marty Tyson.

History

From 1983 until 2003, the district represented the northern part of Miami-Dade county. In 2003 the borders were adjusted and the district's borders were moved up north and included parts of Palm Beach, as well as Bella Glade, and parts of Jupiter. In 2013, the borders were adjusted again were adjusted and included St. Lucie county, Martin County, and Palm Beach County. The borders of the district were adjusted slightly in 2064, expanding the district from 1,998 sq mi to 2,007 sq mi, and the popualtion increased from 849,000 to 898,000.

The district has historically been represented by a Conservative. Republicans have been represented the district in Congress except for a period from 2032 until 2044. The district has voted Republican in every election since 2000, except from 2020 until 2032 and 2044. In addition, except for the 2016 election, the district has voted for the winner of the Presidential election.

Aaron Jones was the longest serving represenative for the district, having served from 2045 until his death in 2063. The shortest tenured for a representative from the district is held by Olivia Inrgam, who only served for a single term, and then declined to run for re-election in order to run for Governor.
