The Toronto Tigers are a professional American football team based in Toronto, Canada. They compete in the National Football League as a member of the American Football Conference (AFC) North Divison. The Tigers play their home games at Toronto Stadium, in Toronto, Canada. The Tigers joined the National Football League in 1966 as the Atlanta Falcons as an expansion team, after the NFL offered then-owner Rankin Smith a franchise to keep him from joining the rival American Football League (AFL).

Founded as the Atlanta Falcons in 1966, the Falcons would play in Atlanta from 1966 until 2021, when they moved to Chicago and became the Chicago Tigers. This would not last long however, as only two seasons later in 2023, the Tigers would move to Toronto and became the Toronto Tigers. During their stint in Chicago, the Tigers shared a Soldier Field with the Chicago Bears. The Tigers would play in the National Football Conference (NFC) South Division from 1966 until 2032, when the NFL re-aligned divisons and the Tigers moved to the AFC North.

Demetrius Turner was the first head coach in franchise history, leading the Tigers from 2020 until midway through the 2030 season. During Turner's tenure, the Tigers would finish first in the NFC South twice (2024, 2025), and would only make the playoffs in those two seasons. After Turner's firing, Assistant Head Coach Charles Railey would take over as head coach. Railey took a 2-7 Tigers team and won 7 straight to finish out the regular season with a 9-7 record. The team would then shock the entire NFL by making it to, and winning a Super Bowl. The 2030 Tigers season is widely considered the greatest single season turn around in NFL history. The Tigers have won three Super Bowl Championships. Charles Railey has lead the Tigers to 3 division titles (1 NFC South title in 2031, and 2 AFC North titles in 2033 and 2034). They have also won two conference championships, and have been in the conference championship in all but one season thus far. Their first was in 2024, when they defeated the Baltimore Ravens, their second was in 2030, when they defeated by the Miami Dolphins, and their most recent was Super Bowl LXVIII, where the Tigers defeated the New York Giants 21-17. Their first Super Bowl title was won under head coach Demetrius Turner, with the other two being won under Charles Railey.

Franchise History

Futher information: Atlanta Falcons, History of the Toronto Tigers

2021: Founding

The Tigers were founded in 2021 and were initally named the Chicago Tigers. The team was founded after the Atlanta Falcons were sold in 2020 and then relocated in 2021. The team was named after the Chicago Tigers who existed the league in 1920. The Tigers would later be renamed the Toronto Tigers.

2020–2030: The Demetrius Turner Era

Main article: Demetrius Turner

Demetrius Turner was the head coach of the Atlanta Falcons from 2020 until 2030, and was the first head coach in franchise history. Turner's tenure as head coach was underwhelimg, with his team missing the playoffs eight of his ten full seasons as head coach, with his team failing to make the playoffs in his final four seasons. Turner would then be fired following a 2-7 start to the 2030 season. At the time of his firing, Turner had a record of 72-95-1 in the regular season, and a 5-1 record in the postseason.

2020–2021

The first two seasons under first year Head Coach Demetrius Turner were rough. The Falcons would go 3-13 in 2020 and would start out 3-1 in the 2021 season. The Falcons would then lose the 10 out of the next 12 games following their bye week finishing at a disappointing 5-11, and placing 4th in the NFC South again.

2022–2023: The Chicago Tigers Era

In their first season in Chicago, the Tigers take a step back finishing at 6-10 and 3rd in the NFC South. The Tigers would also start rookie quarterback Dylan Alexander after starting QB Tyler Huntley posted a record of 2-3 in the first five games.

2024: First Super Bowl Championship

The 2024 season was the first season for the Tigers in Toronto. The Tigers began the season red hot going 6-1 by their bye in week 8. The Tigers would only lose 2 more games during the course of the season, fininishing 13-3 and winning the NFC South for the first time. Khalil Mack also won Defensive Player of the Year, which was the first time a Tigers player ever won a league award.

In the Divisional round the Tigers would narrowly edge out the visiting Chicago Bears in a close 24-17 victory. The following week, the Tigers would face their division rivals in the NFC Championship Game. The Tigers would once again manage to scrape out a win, and would make their first trip to the Super Bowl in franchise history. In the Super Bowl, the Tigers would defeat the AFC Champion Baltimore Ravens in a blowout 66-31 win, winning their first ever Super Bowl title. Running back J.K. Dobbins was named the Super Bowl Most Valuable Player, marking the first time a running back has won the award since 1998 when Terrell Davis was named MVP in Super Bowl XXXII.

2025–2029

The 2025 season would fail to see the Tigers defend their title, going 10-6 and losing to the Carolina Panthers in the NFC Championship. The 2026 NFC Championship game was the final playoff game under head coach Demetrius Turner.

The Tigers would fail to make the playoff in the final four seasons under head coach Demetrius Turner. The Tigers would finish 2nd in the NFC South in the 2026 season with a record of 7-9. However, the 2026 season saw the emergence of eventual Hall of Fame edge rusher Evan Graham, who won Defensive Player of the Year as well as Edge Rusher of the Year. 2027 saw the Tigers bottom out yet again at 3-13. Rookie Wide Receiever Earl Wake would win Offensive Rookie of the Year.

2028 would be a bounce back year for the Tigers, going 7-9, and narrowly missing the playoffs. Quarterback Dylan Alexander would have his best statistical season with 5,120 passing yards, 49 touchdowns and 19 interceptions, and taking home the NFL Most Valuable Player award for the first and only time in his career. Evan Graham would also win his second Defensive Player of the Year Awards as well as making his fifth straight Pro Bowl, and second consecutive First Team All-Pro nod.

The beginning of the 2029 season was disaster for the Tigers. The team would go 2-7 under Demetrius Turner, who would be fired durign the team's bye week. The team would then name Charles Railey as Interim Head Coach for the remainder of the 2029 season.

2030–Present: The Charles Railey Era

2030: Second Super Bowl Champinship

Charles Railey was named interim head coach of the Toronto Tigers after former HC Demetrius Turner was fired. The Tigers would surprise everyone winning all seven of their games down the stretch and making the playoffs with a 9-7 record.

The Tigers would shut out the Rams in the Wild Card round 31-0. They would then win in a narrow 2 possesion game over the division rival Buccaneers. In the NFC Championship, the Tigers faced the Arizona Cardinals at State Farm Stadium. The Tigers were expected to lose this game due to their 2-7 record at the start of the season, however the Tigers would shock everyone when they blew out the Cardinals in a 41-21 win; sealing their second trip to the Super Bowl.

In Super Bowl LXIV, Charles Railey would bench vetern QB Dylan Alexander in favor of rookie college phenomn Ciante Winston due to an injury sustained by Alexander in the NFC Championship. Winston would have an outstanding first career start posting 420 passing yards, 210 rushing yards, completing 65.5% of his passes, and throwing 5 touchdowns, no interceptions, with an additional 2 rushing touchdown in a 56-23 victory. For his efforts, Winston was named Super Bowl MVP.

Stadiums

The Tigers have played in five stadiums over their 68 year history. The first stadium was the Atlanta–Fulton County Stadium, sharing with the Atlanta Braves Major League Baseball team until 1991. In 1992, the Georgia Dome was built, and the Falcons played there from its opening to the 2016 season. The Dome has been frequently used for college football, including Georgia State football and college bowl games such as the Peach Bowl.

In an effort to replace the aging Georgia Dome and potentially host a future Super Bowl, team owner Arthur Blank proposed a deal with the city of Atlanta to build a new state-of-the-art stadium not far from where the Georgia Dome is located. Blank will contribute $800 million and the city of Atlanta will contribute an additional $200 million via bonds backed by the city's hotel/motel tax towards the construction of a retractable roof stadium. Blank will contribute additional money for cost overruns if it is needed. The team will provide up to $50 million towards infrastructure costs that weren't included in the construction budget and to retire the remaining debt on the Georgia Dome. In addition, Blank's foundation and the city will each provide $15 million for development in surrounding neighborhoods. Though the total cost of the stadium was initially estimated to be around $1 billion, the total cost was revised to $1.5 billion according to Blank. In March 2013, the Atlanta City Council voted 11–4 in favor of building the stadium. The retractable roof Mercedes-Benz Stadium broke ground in May 2014, and became the third home stadium for the Falcons and the first for the new Atlanta United FC Major League Soccer club upon opening in 2017.

When the Falcons moved to Chicago and became the Tigers, the Tigers would lease Solider Field during their stay until the stadium in Toronto was finished in 2023. The Tigers would then move to the Toronto Stadium in Toronto, Canada. The project for the stadium began in 2019, shortly after Arthur Blank sold the team to Hyojin Choi. Choi made a deal with the city of Toronto for a $1.13 trillion dollar stadium planned to seat a maximm of 89,489 people. The City Council of Toronto voted overwhelimngly in favor of the motion, and the stadium began construction in the following months. The stadium was completed on February 4th, 2022, and the Tigers would move to the new stadium in the following season.

Logo and Uniforms

Current uniforms

The Toronto Tigers' uniforms are orange, black, white and grey. The home uniforms include an orange jersey with orange pants. The helmet on both home and away uniforms are black with the Tigers logo on both sides of the helmet. The shoulders on both home and away jersey utilize a tiger print pattern on the front and back of the shoulder pads. On the home jersey kit, players' numbers are displayed in white with a black outline. On the away jerseys the number is colored in grey with an orange accent. Both home and away jerseys use both grey pants and socks.
