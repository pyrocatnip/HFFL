Hyojin Choi (Korean: 최효진; RR: 'Choi Hyojin'; pronounced: Choi-HEEYo-jin; Born: December 20, 1987) is a Korean born American football quarterback for the Los Angeles Rams of the National Football League. He was drafted in 2021 by the Tampa Bay Buccaneers with the sixth overall pick. He would spend his first two seasons with the Buccaneers before being traded in the 2022 offseason to the Rams. Choi attended Rosewood State University. He would win the Heisman Trophy in his final season with the Rosas, and defeated the USC Trojans in the 2020 College Football National Championship game.

In his first season, Choi threw for a record breaking 5,280 yards with 49 touchdowns and 14 interceptions. He led the league in passer rating, completion percentage, passing touchdowns, rush attemopts by a quarterback, and rushing yards by a quarterback. He also won multiple awards including NFL MVP, Offensive Player of the Year, Offensive Rookie of the Year, and the Bart Bell Award. In his sophomore season, Choi set the record for the most passing yards (6,256) and passing touchdowns (66) in a season, and would again league in completions, completion percentage, passing yards, and passing touchdowns. He take home is second NFL MVP award, and was named to the All-Pro team and Pro Bowl for his second straight season. In the offseason Choi was traded to the Los Angeles Rams. In his first season with the Rams, he would take the team all the way to the Super Bowl defeating the Baltimore Ravens in Super Bowl LVIII and taking home Super Bowl MVP. Choi would win his third straight NFL MVP award and was again a First Team All-Pro.

Early Life

Hyojin Choi was born in Suwon, Korea. He moved to the United States at the age of 2 years old. He attended Rosewood Academy, where he played quarterback. He led the Trojans to a 10-2 record in his sophomore season, and was named Maryland High School Breakout Player of the Year award in his junior season. He would attend Rosewood State University, a private research university located in Union City, Maryland.

High School Career

Choi played for three seasons with the Rosewood Academy Trojans. He threw for a career total of 21,389 yards, a career average of 5.5 yards per game, and 113 career touchdowns to only 37 interceptions. He was named the Maryland High School Athlete of the year in his sophomore season, where he threw for 3,210 yards, 26 touchdowns and only 1 interception. In the following season, Choi led the Trojans to a 10-2 record and would win the Maryland High School Breakout Player of the Year award. He would also lead the Trojans to their first ever High School Football National Championship title. In his final season, Choi threw for only 2,771 yards, 12 touchdowns and 4 interceptions.

Recruiting

Choi was unanimously agreed as the number 1 quarterback prospect from the state of Maryland for the 2017 class. He was recruited by numerous big name colleges including Alabama, Clemson, Ohio State, Michigan, Notre Dame, USC, and UCLA. He also recieved offers from local colleges such as the University of Maryland, the University of North Carolina, and the University of Virginia. However, Choi would end up decidng to remain close to home and was accepted to Rosewood State University.

Professional Career

Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2021-2022)

Choi would have an outstanding rookie season with the Buccaneers individually, however the team would only win 6 games and finished last in the NFC South. He finished the season with a then record breaking 5,820 yards and completed nearly 70% of his passes. He also threw for 49 touchdowns to only 14 interceptions. Choi also showed his dual threat capabilities with 145 rush attempts for 1,326, 9.1 yards per carry, 13 touchdowns and 0 fumbles. For his efforts, Choi won NFL MVP, Offensive Player of the Year, and Offensive Rookie of the Year. This was the first time in NFL history that a rookie player won all three of these awards. Choi also led the league in passer rating, completion percentage, touchdowns, QB rush attempts, and rushing yards.

2022 would see improvement for the Buccaneers, going 11-5 and winning the NFC South. For his part, Choi set the record for the most passing yards in a single season with 6,256. He also had 66 touchdowns and only 13 interceptions. He led the league again in passer rating, completion percent, yards, and touchdowns; though he would fail to rush for over 1,000 yards. In his first playoff game, Choi had a poor showing with a career low passer rating of 97.6. He also threw 2 touchdowns and 2 interceptions and 331 passing yards in a wild cards loss to the Arizona Cardinals. The offseason saw being traded to Choi the Los Angeles Rams.

Los Angeles Rams (2023-Present)

In his third season in the NFL and first with the Rams, Choi had another outstanding individual season. He threw for 5,587 yards 53 touchdowns and 14 interceptions, and completed a career high of 73.2% of his passes. The Rams would win 13 games clinching the NFC West and giving the Rams a bye to the Divisonal Round. In the Divisional Round, he would lead his team to a blowout victory over the New York Giants. He would then lead his team to victory in the NFC Championship before making his first Super Bowl trip. Choi was once again named NFL MVP for the third straight year.

In his second season with the Rams, Choi led the team to a perfect 16-0 record and would earn the number 1 seed in the NFC for the second straight season. He would also improve in his individual stats; throwing for 6,148 yards, a record 78 touchdowns, and only 10 interceptions. He also had 136 rush attempts and another record setting 1,759 rushing yards. He also led the league in passer rating, completion percentage, yards and touchdowns for the four straight season.

Through the first 11 weeks the Rams were 10-1. The season would then derail for the Rmas as they would lose Anthony McFarland in week 11, they would also lose Kendrick Reed in week 12, Patrick Qyeen and Kasey Crowder went down in week 14 and then lost Patrick Queen in Week 15. Through that period, the Rams would lose all of their final five games going 10-6. The Rams would then face the Packers in the Wild Card round, who they had just lost to a week earlier in a narrow 41-44 loss. The Rams would not be able to rally into a win losing 24-27 ending their season. In the Wild Card game, the Rams would lose another running back to injury with Jaleel Sullivan going down with a dislocated anckle. Choi ended the season with 5,477 yards, 59 touchdowns and only 8 interceptions. He completed 372 out of 512 of his passing attempts, good for a completion percentage of 72%. He would also be sacked 21 times, which is the second highest sacks in his career. The season continued Choi's streak of never having a passing for less than 5,000 yards in a season. He also led the league in passing yards, passing touchdowns, and had the 4th highest passer rating.
