    Hyojin Choi (Korean: 최효진; RR: 'Choi Hyojin'; pronounced: Choi-HEEYo-jin; Born: December 20, 1987) is a Korean born American football quarterback for the Buffalo Bills of the National Football League. He was drafted in 2008 in the third round by the Buccaneers. Choi attended Rosewood State University.

    Early Life

    Hyojin Choi was born in Suwon, Korea. He moved to the United States at the age of 2 years old. He attended Rosewood Academy, where he played quarterback. He led the Trojans to a 10-2 record in his sophomore season, and was named Maryland High School Breakout Player of the Year award in his junior season.

    NFL Draft

    Main: 2007 NFL Draft

    Choi was ranked as the 4-5th best quarterback in the class, and was expected to go in the later rounds of the 2007 NFL draft. Analysts were impressed by his speed, running a 4.38 40-yard dash, however concerns over potential injuries due to his style of quarterback play and questions regarding his ability to read defenses prevented him from being seen as higher than a third round pick. Scouts worried that frequent injuries due to the hard-hitting nature of NFL defenses would negatively affect Choi's explosivness on the ground. Other major media figures believed that Choi should switch to runningback or wide receiever because of his speed and ability to evade tackles.

    Professional Career

    Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2007-Present)

    2007 Season

    Main: 2007 Tampa Bay Buccaneers season

    Choi was named the Buccaneers starter for the 2007 season. He would lead the Buccaneers to a touchdown on his first drive in the NFL against the Seahawks. Choi led the Buccaneers to a 62-34 blowout win in Seattle, where he passed for 516 yards, was 25-37 on passes, 6 touchdowns, 3 sacks, and rushed for an additional 100 yards and 2 rushing touchdowns. In week 7, the Buccaneers were upset by the then 0-6 Detroit Lions in overtime by a score of 38-44. The Buccaneers were down 31-36 with a minute left, and Choi would lead the Buccaneers down the field and score with only 3 seconds left in the game. The upset occurred thanks to a 86 yard punt return by Detroit, as time expired. In that game, Choi posted a perfect quarterback passing rating of 158.3, the first time a quarterback has posted a perfect passer rating in his rookie season.

    Choi had an outstanding performance in the eighth game of the season. He scored nine total touchdowns, 5 on the ground and 4 in the air. He rushed for 268 yards and had over 300 passing yards.
