Cam Hutton (Born: December 9th, 2004) is an American football quarterback for the Rosewood State Rosas. He is the current Heisman Trophy winner, and led the Rosas to consecutive Colllege Football National Championships. Hutton was only the second quarterback in Rosewood State history to rush for over 1,000 yards in a season, and was the first to do it since Scott Odom in 2004. Hutton will be entering his junior season in 2023. Hutton was the first quarterback to lead the Rosas to consecutive undefeated seasons.

Early Life

Hutton was born in Pasadena, California on December 9th, 2004. His family moved to Los Angeles when Hutton only 3 years old. He attended Frida Kahlo High School in Los Angeles, Califonria.

High School Career

Hutton attended Frida Kahlo High School, and played both football and was a runneer on the varsity track and field team. Hutton threw for 17,491 yards in his high school career and became the first quarterback in school history to rush for over 1,000 yards. Hutton was allegedly able to throw a football up to 75 yards, and had a 40 time of 4.38. Hutton his high school to a state championship in his final year of high school, and would win MVP.

Recruiting

Hutton was the top target of 2021. He was a five-star quarterback and was expected to commit to a top program such as Alabama, LSU, Ohio State, Clemson, or Notre Dame. Early on into his decision process, Hutton ruled out any big name school, preferring to attend a lesser-known program. Hutton recieved offers from Auburn, Rosewood State, Nebraska, Washington State, UCLA, USC and Yale,

College Career

2021 Season

Hutton had a breakout freshman season. He threw for 3,680 yards, completed 338 of 493 passes (68.8%), threw for 39 touchdowns and 10 interceptions. Hutton had a touchdown percentage of 7.9% and an interception percentage of only 2%. Hutton also nearly broke the record for the most single season rushing yards by a quarterback in Rosewood State history, rushing for 1,261 yards, which was only 52 rushing yards away from tying with Scott Odom's 1,313 rushing yards in his 2004 National Championship campaign. In the postseason, Hutton threw for 8 touchdowns and no interceptions, which was the first time in Rosewood State history that a quarterback threw no interceptions in the postseason. Hutton also led the Rosas to their first undefeated season since 2004.
